## Core Global Platforms (General & Mass Reach)

- [Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/) – The largest platform; essential for broad reach, targeted advertising, and massive interest-based Groups.
- [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/) – Dominant video platform; the world's second-largest search engine.
- [Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/) – Visual platform focused on high-quality images, short videos (Reels), and creator collaboration.
- [TikTok](https://www.tiktok.com/) – Leading short-form video platform driven by the "For You" discovery algorithm.
- [Snapchat](https://www.snapchat.com/) – Image and video messaging platform popular with Gen Z, featuring disappearing content and AR filters.
- [X (formerly Twitter)](https://x.com/) – Microblogging platform emphasizing real-time news, trending updates, and public conversations.
- [Threads](https://www.threads.net/) – Meta's text-based platform, directly integrated with Instagram for rapid audience building.
- [Pinterest](https://www.pinterest.com/) – Visual discovery and bookmarking engine, highly effective for product discovery and inspiration.

## Professional, B2B & Q&A Networks

- [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/) – Professional networking platform for B2B, career development, and industry thought leadership.
- [Quora](https://www.quora.com/) – Q&A platform for demonstrating subject-matter expertise and driving high-intent traffic.
- [Stack Overflow](https://stackoverflow.com/) – Q&A site primarily for programmers and developers (essential for technical companies).
- [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/) – Social network for scientists and researchers to share papers and ask technical questions.
- [Crunchbase](https://www.crunchbase.com/) – Platform for networking and finding information on private and public companies.
- [Viadeo](https://www.viadeo.com/) – Professional social networking site popular in Europe.
- [Xing](https://www.xing.com/) – Professional networking site dominant in the DACH region (Germany, Austria, Switzerland).

## Messaging & Private Communities

- [WhatsApp](https://www.whatsapp.com/) – Global encrypted messaging; essential for customer service and small group communication.
- [Telegram](https://telegram.org/) – Cloud-based messaging prioritizing speed, large channels, and secure communication.
- [Discord](https://discord.com/) – Community platform (servers) for private creator, brand, and niche hobby communities.
- [Viber](https://www.viber.com/) – Messaging and VoIP app popular in Eastern Europe and parts of Asia.
- [Signal](https://signal.org/) – Encrypted messaging app known for extreme privacy and security.
- [Line](https://line.me/) – Popular messaging and social network in Japan and Southeast Asia.
- [Skype](https://www.skype.com/) – Communication platform for video calls, voice calls, and instant messaging.
- [Facebook Messenger](https://www.messenger.com/) – Messaging app for conversations tied to the Facebook ecosystem.

## Regional & Asian Dominant Platforms

- [WeChat (Weixin)](https://www.wechat.com/) – All-in-one messaging, social, and payment platform dominant in China.
- [Douyin](https://www.douyin.com/) – The Chinese version of TikTok, massive for short-form video in mainland China.
- [Kuaishou](https://www.kuaishou.com/) – Major Chinese video sharing and live-streaming app (often referred to as 'Kwai' outside China).
- [Sina Weibo](https://weibo.com/) – Leading Chinese microblogging service, similar to a hybrid of X and Facebook.
- [QQ](https://im.qq.com/) – Instant messaging service and social platform popular among younger users in China.
- [Qzone](https://qzone.qq.com/) – Social networking service popular in China, integrated with QQ.
- [VK (Vkontakte)](https://vk.com/) – Russia's largest social media platform, similar in function to Facebook.
- [Odnoklassniki (OK.ru)](https://ok.ru/) – Russian social network focused on connecting old school friends and colleagues.
- [Naver BAND](https://band.us/) – Korean mobile community app for group communication and coordination.
- [KakaoTalk](https://www.kakaocorp.com/page/service/service/KakaoTalk) – Dominant messenger app in South Korea.
- [Xiaohongshu (RedNote)](https://www.xiaohongshu.com/) – Chinese platform focused on fashion, beauty, and travel content (similar to Instagram/Pinterest).

## Niche, Hobby & Interest Networks

- [Reddit](https://www.reddit.com/) – Categorized by subreddits, covering every niche imaginable (e.g., r/DIY, r/stocks).
- [Letterboxd](https://letterboxd.com/) – Social network for film lovers to review, track, and share movie preferences.
- [Goodreads](https://www.goodreads.com/) – Social network for book lovers to track reading, share reviews, and join book clubs.
- [Ravelry](https://www.ravelry.com/) – Community and database for knitters, crocheters, spinners, and weavers.
- [Strava](https://www.strava.com/) – Social network for runners, cyclists, and fitness enthusiasts to track and share activities.
- [Nextdoor](https://nextdoor.com/) – Hyper-local social network connecting neighbors for community news and local business recommendations.
- [Twitch](https://www.twitch.tv/) – Primarily for live streaming games, but increasingly used for creative content, music, and software development.
- [Dribbble](https://dribbble.com/) – Platform for showcasing design work, UI/UX, and illustrations.
- [Behance](https://www.behance.net/) – Portfolio platform owned by Adobe for creatives across various media.
- [Untappd](https://untappd.com/) – Social network for beer enthusiasts to track, rate, and discover new beers.
- [Filmow](https://filmow.com/) – Brazilian social network for sharing and reviewing movies.
- [MyFitnessPal](https://www.myfitnesspal.com/) – Food and exercise tracking app with social features for motivation.
- [Houzz](https://www.houzz.com/) – Platform for home renovation and design, connecting homeowners and professionals.

## Web3 & Decentralized Social Networks (User-Owned)

- [Mastodon](https://joinmastodon.org/) – **Federated** microblogging network (Fediverse) where users join independent, user-run servers.
- [Farcaster](https://www.farcaster.xyz/) – Decentralized social protocol that allows users to own their identity and data, supporting multiple client apps (e.g., Warpcast).
- [Lens Protocol](https://www.lens.xyz/) – Decentralized social graph protocol built on Polygon, where content and connections are NFTs owned by the user.
- [Bluesky](https://bsky.app/) – Decentralized social networking protocol (AT Protocol) created by the co-founder of Twitter.
- [Minds](https://www.minds.com/) – Open-source and decentralized platform that rewards users with tokens for engagement.
- [Mirror](https://mirror.xyz/) – Web3 platform for writers to publish long-form content, tokenize it as NFTs, and run crowdfunding.
- [Steemit](https://steemit.com/) – Pioneering Web3 social platform that rewards users with cryptocurrency (STEEM) for posting and curating content.
- [Diamond App (DeSo)](https://diamondapp.com/) – Decentralized social media built on the DeSo blockchain, enabling monetization of content.
- [Plexo](https://plexo.app/) – Decentralized social feed built on the Lens Protocol.
- [Peepeth](https://www.peepeth.com/) – Microblogging platform built on the Ethereum blockchain.
- [Audius](https://audius.co/) – Decentralized music streaming and sharing platform governed by creators and listeners.
